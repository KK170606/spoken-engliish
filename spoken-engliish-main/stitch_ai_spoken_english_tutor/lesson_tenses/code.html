Past Simple vs. Present Perfect

The Past Simple is used for actions completed at a specific time in the past. The Present Perfect connects the past to the present, focusing on the experience rather than the specific time.

Past Simple

"I visited London in 2019."

Specific time mentioned.
Present Perfect

"I have visited London three times."

Focus on the experience.

Common Examples

check_circle volume_up

She finished her homework an hour ago.

Past Simple: Finished (Completed Action)

check_circle volume_up

Have you ever eaten Japanese food?

Present Perfect: Life Experience
