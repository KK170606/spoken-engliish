Unit 4: Verb Tenses

Past Simple vs. Present Perfect

65% complete

menu_book Rule

Past Simple is used for actions completed at a specific time in the past. Present Perfect connects the past to the present and focuses on experience.

Past Simple

"I visited London in 2019."

Specific time mentioned.
Present Perfect

"I have visited London three times."

Focus on the experience.

Mini-Practice

"I _____ (see) that movie yesterday."
